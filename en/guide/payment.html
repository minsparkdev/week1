Payment King - Bill Payer Decider

"Who pays?" - Settle this question fairly with a fun game!

What is Payment King?

Payment King is a game that decides who pays the bill after dining with friends in a fun and fair way. No more awkward wallet-reaching or silent staring contests. Just spin the roulette or play ladder game, and today's Payment King is automatically chosen!

Supports 2 to 8 participants, with two game modes to choose from. Since it's completely random, no one can complain about unfairness.

When to Use

  • After company dinners - When it's awkward to ask who's treating
  • Dining with friends - When you chose "taking turns" over splitting the bill
  • Date expenses - When you feel bad having one person always pay
  • Club/group gatherings - Deciding this month's treasurer
  • Office coffee runs - Deciding who buys coffee after lunch

How to Play

  1. Click the üí∞ Payment King card on the What to Eat main screen.
  2. Select the number of participants (2-8 people).
  3. Enter each participant's name.
  4. Choose the game mode (Roulette or Ladder).
  5. Press start and the game begins!
  6. When results come out, the Payment King is announced! üéâ

Roulette vs Ladder Game

üé° Roulette is a thrilling game with flashy visual effects. Enjoy the suspense of watching the spinning wheel while hoping "Please not me!" Recommended when you want quick decisions.

ü™ú Ladder Game is the traditional, familiar method. Each person picks a starting point, then the ladder draws itself revealing the result. Enjoy wondering "Where is my line going?"

Guaranteed Fairness

Payment King uses a completely random algorithm. Regardless of who entered their name first or which position they're in, all participants have equal probability of being selected.

Object to the result? Just run it again! But accepting the outcome is the true spirit of bill games. üòé

FAQ

Q. How many people can participate?
A. Minimum 2 to maximum 8 people can participate. For more, we recommend splitting into two teams or having preliminaries. üòÑ

Q. What if the same person keeps winning?
A. Just unlucky... But statistically, consecutive wins are very rare. If it happens, maybe buy a lottery ticket? (Reverse luck might hit!)

Q. Can I save the results?
A. Screenshot the result screen or use the share function. Keeping "evidence" can prevent future "I paid last time!" disputes.
