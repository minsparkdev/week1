Korean Soul Food: Everything About Kimchi Stew

January 12, 2026 Â· 5 min read

Korea's Eternal Soul Food

Have you ever found yourself saying "Let's just get kimchi jjigae" when you couldn't decide what to eat? For Koreans, kimchi jjigae is more than just food. It embodies the warmth of home cooking, a mother's touch, and the very essence of Korean identity.

According to surveys, kimchi jjigae consistently ranks as Koreans' favorite stew. Loved by people of all ages and generations, what makes this dish so irresistible?

The History of Kimchi Jjigae

The history of kimchi jjigae is intertwined with the history of kimchi itself. Records show that vegetables were preserved in salt since the Three Kingdoms period, and with the introduction of red pepper powder during the Joseon Dynasty, the familiar red kimchi we know today was born.

Kimchi jjigae as a dish is estimated to have emerged in the late Joseon period. It began when people started cooking fermented kimchi into a soup rather than eating it as is. As pork became more widely available, "pork kimchi jjigae" became the most common form today.

Why Kimchi Jjigae is Special

There are several reasons why kimchi jjigae has become Korea's soul food.

1. A Symbol of Home Cooking

Kimchi jjigae is made in most Korean households. Each family's version tastes slightly different, carrying a mother's personal touch that can't be replicated anywhere else. That's why you'll hear people say, "My mom's kimchi jjigae is the best in the world."

2. The Deep Flavor of Fermentation

The secret to kimchi jjigae's taste lies in well-fermented kimchi. The organic acids and amino acids produced during fermentation create a deep, complex flavor profile. It's common knowledge among Koreans that the more sour the kimchi, the better the stew.

3. The Comfort of Warm Broth

Hot soup dishes are central to Korean food culture. Especially on cold winter days, one spoonful of steaming kimchi jjigae warms both body and soul. This is the comfort that kimchi jjigae provides.

4. The Perfect Rice Companion

While kimchi jjigae is delicious on its own, it truly shines when paired with rice. Whether you mix the rice into the spicy broth or enjoy spoonfuls of meat and vegetables with rice, the combination is irreplaceable.

Types of Kimchi Jjigae

Beyond the basic pork kimchi jjigae, there are many variations.

  • Tuna Kimchi Jjigae: A quick version made with canned tuna. A staple for college students living alone.
  • Ham Kimchi Jjigae (Budae-style): Made heartier with ham and sausages.
  • Tofu Kimchi Jjigae: A lighter version with plenty of tofu instead of meat.
  • Mackerel Pike Kimchi Jjigae: A special variation with canned mackerel pike for deeper flavor.
  • Aged Kimchi Jjigae: Made with long-fermented kimchi for an even richer taste.

Tips for Delicious Kimchi Jjigae

Here are some tips to make your kimchi jjigae even more delicious.

  1. Use sour kimchi: Slightly fermented kimchi makes the best stew.
  2. Stir-fry the kimchi first: Sauteing the kimchi in oil before adding liquid enhances umami.
  3. Use pork belly: The right amount of fat from pork belly enriches the broth.
  4. Use stock: Using anchovy or kelp stock instead of water adds depth.
  5. Add a touch of sugar: If the kimchi is too sour, a little sugar balances the flavor.
  6. Add green onions last: Add them just before turning off the heat to preserve their aroma.

How to Choose a Good Kimchi Jjigae Restaurant

What should you look for when eating kimchi jjigae out?

  • Quality of kimchi: Look for places that use homemade kimchi.
  • Meat quality: Places that use generous amounts of fresh pork.
  • Freshly cooked: Restaurants that cook it to order taste better.
  • Side dishes: The quality of a kimchi jjigae restaurant shows in its banchan.

Perfect Pairings with Kimchi Jjigae

While kimchi jjigae is perfect on its own, some additions make it even better.

  • Bowl of rice: An absolute must. Hot rice is essential.
  • Fried egg: Top the stew with a sunny-side-up egg and break the runny yolk for bliss.
  • Ramyeon noodles: Add instant noodles to the remaining broth for a satisfying finish.
  • Soju: A classic adult pairing. A glass of soju with kimchi jjigae is timeless.

Conclusion: A Dish That Never Disappoints

Kimchi jjigae isn't flashy. It doesn't require expensive ingredients or complicated techniques. But that's what makes it special. Made from common ingredients that every household has, by anyone who knows how to cook, yet its taste and meaning are irreplaceable to Koreans.

If you're unsure what to have for lunch today, how about kimchi jjigae? It's a choice you can never go wrong with.
